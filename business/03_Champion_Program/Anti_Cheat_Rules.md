# Anti-Cheat Rules

Champion Program has cash on the line, so we treat integrity seriously from cohort 1.

## Day-1 baseline rules

- **Photo deadline:** Day-1 progress photo must be uploaded by 11:59 PM of Day 1. Late photos = ineligible for Champion (still in cohort).
- **Photo metadata:** Phone EXIF metadata is preserved on upload. We check for date manipulation.
- **Weight and waist:** Submitted with a short selfie video showing the scale/measuring tape. Coach reviews 100% of Day-1 video evidence.

## Mid-cohort

- **Suspicious daily check-ins:** If a user submits 21 perfect check-ins on Day 21 (i.e. backfilling), it's flagged. Backfilled days don't count for Consistency score.
- **Coach's red flag:** Any coach can flag a user for suspected fabrication. Flagged users go through additional video review for Champion eligibility.

## Day-21 submission

- **Photo consistency:** Day-21 photo must match Day-1 lighting, framing, and clothing reasonably. We don't expect perfect studio match, but obvious lighting tricks are flagged.
- **Random verification:** Top 5 finalists get a 5-min Zoom call with founder to verify weight, waist, and reflect on their journey. This becomes content with permission.

## Disqualification protocol

If we find evidence of fabrication:
1. User is disqualified from Champion ranking
2. User is allowed to complete cohort with no public note
3. User receives a 50% refund as goodwill (we don't want to make enemies)
4. User is not invited to future cohorts
5. No public shaming — ever.

## What we do not do

- We do not require gym/clinic verification (too high friction for ₹999 cohort)
- We do not run body fat / DEXA tests (too expensive)
- We do not penalize natural variance (water weight, time-of-day weighing)

## What we do trust by default

- The vast majority of users are honest. The rules above are 95% deterrent and 5% enforcement.
- Coach's gut is usually right. If a coach flags someone after 21 days of daily interaction, take it seriously.

## Edge case: medication-driven weight loss

If a user is on weight-loss medication (GLP-1 etc.), they must disclose it on the assessment. Their Champion eligibility is **not removed**, but the Physical dimension is capped at 50% (15/30 max) so they don't out-compete habit-driven users. This rule is published transparently in the Champion rules.
