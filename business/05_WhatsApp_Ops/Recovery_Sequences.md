# Recovery Sequences — When a User Misses

Missed days are the moment retention is won or lost. The wrong message ("you missed yesterday!") triggers the guilt cycle and they uninstall. The right message keeps them in.

---

## Trigger logic

| Days missed | Trigger | Sender |
|---|---|---|
| 1 missed day | Soft nudge | Auto |
| 2 missed days | Personal DM | Coach |
| 3+ missed days | Personal call (WhatsApp voice) | Coach |
| Day 7 + 4+ missed | Founder voice note | Founder |

---

## 1 day missed — Soft Nudge (auto, next morning)

> Hey [first name], didn't see you yesterday.
> No guilt. Today is Day [X].
> Just reply with one emoji and we keep going. 💪

---

## 2 days missed — Personal DM (coach, second missed evening)

> [First name], two quiet days. I'm not asking you to make up for them.
> Tell me — what's getting in the way?
> Work? Travel? Just tired?
>
> We'll adjust. The plan moves with your week.

---

## 3+ days missed — Voice note (coach, day 3 evening)

Coach records a 30–60 sec voice note. Script:

> Hey [first name], it's [coach name]. Just leaving a quick voice instead of a text.
> No pressure, no judgment. I just want to know — are you okay?
> If you want to keep going, we'll restart you on a lighter version. If you want to pause, that's also fine.
> Send me a message when you can.

---

## Day-7-and-4-missed — Founder voice note

> Hey [first name], [founder name] here.
> Most people would have written you off by now. We don't.
> 14 days left. Here's the deal: I'll personally help you finish — even if you do half of what's prescribed.
> Reply yes and your coach will set up a lighter plan tonight.

## Specific scenario sequences

### Scenario A: Sick / unwell

User says: "I'm not well, fever / cold / period / migraine."

Coach reply:
> Rest is the priority. No workouts till you're 80%.
> Just: water, sleep, a protein meal, and one daily reply (even just "still resting").
> We'll resume the workout track when you're back.

### Scenario B: Travel

User says: "I'm travelling for work."

Coach reply:
> Travel mode for [N] days:
> - 8,000 steps target
> - 1 hotel-room workout (10-min): [URL]
> - Plate method at every meal you can control
> - One daily check-in
>
> No new workouts till you're back. Your streak doesn't break for travel.

### Scenario C: Family event / festival

User says: "Wedding / Diwali / family function."

Coach reply:
> Festivals are practice, not punishment.
> Two rules for the day:
> - Eat slowly, one plate, no second round
> - 5,000 steps minimum (count the dance floor)
> Tomorrow we go back to plan. No guilt.

### Scenario D: Wants to quit

User says: "I think I'm done. This isn't working for me."

Coach reply (within 30 min):
> Heard. Before we close out — one short call?
> Just 10 minutes. I want to understand what didn't work, even if you're done.
> [Calendly link]

If they decline: refund per policy + ask one survey question: "What would have made this work?"

### Scenario E: Asking for medical advice

User says: anything about diabetes / thyroid / PCOS / blood test / medication.

Coach reply:
> That's a doctor question, not a coach question.
> What I can do: keep your habit floor steady (water, walks, protein, sleep). Send me whatever your doctor advises and I'll work around it.

---

## Anti-pattern messages — never send these

❌ "You missed yesterday. Don't give up!"
❌ "Where were you? The cohort is moving on."
❌ "You paid ₹999 — make it count."
❌ "Other users are ahead of you."
❌ "If you skip today you'll lose the streak."
❌ Anything in ALL CAPS.
❌ Anything with > 1 emoji.

---

## What we measure

- 1-day-miss recovery rate (target ≥ 60% return next day)
- 3-day-miss recovery rate (target ≥ 30%)
- Quit rate by day (target curve: < 5% by Day 7, < 15% by Day 21)
- NPS impact of recovery messages (post-cohort survey)
